Sample Novel - Rough Draft

Chapter 1: The Beginning

The morning sun crept through the dusty windows of the old bookstore. Sarah wiped down the counter, preparing for another quiet day. Little did she know that today would change everything.

A stranger entered, carrying a leather-bound journal. "I need your help," he said urgently. "This book contains secrets that could save the world—or destroy it."

Sarah's hands trembled as she took the journal. The pages were filled with strange symbols and maps she'd never seen before. Whatever this was, it was far beyond her simple life as a bookkeeper.

"Who are you?" she asked.

"Someone who's been searching for you for a very long time," the stranger replied.

Chapter 2: The Discovery

Later that evening, Sarah sat alone in her apartment, studying the journal by candlelight. The symbols began to make sense—they were coordinates, pointing to locations all over the world.

Each location marked a place where something had been hidden, something powerful. But the journal didn't explain what these objects were, only that they needed to be found before "they" discovered them.

Sarah realized she had a choice to make. She could return the journal, go back to her normal life, forget this ever happened. Or she could follow the coordinates and discover the truth.

The decision wasn't easy. Her life was simple, safe, predictable. But something inside her yearned for more.

She made her choice.

Chapter 3: The First Journey

Two days later, Sarah stood at the entrance to an ancient temple in Peru. The first coordinates had led her here, to a place forgotten by time.

The temple walls were covered in the same symbols from the journal. She traced her fingers over them, feeling a strange energy pulsing beneath the stone.

In the center of the main chamber, she found it—a crystal orb, glowing with an inner light. As soon as she touched it, visions flooded her mind. She saw the past, the present, possible futures. She saw the stranger who gave her the journal, fighting against dark forces.

And she saw herself, standing at a crossroads, holding the fate of humanity in her hands.

The orb grew hot in her palm, and she knew there was no turning back now.

Chapter 4: The Revelation

Back in her hotel room, Sarah examined the orb more closely. It seemed to respond to her thoughts, showing her images and information when she focused on specific questions.

She learned about an ancient civilization that had possessed incredible knowledge. They had hidden their most powerful artifacts around the world, protected by puzzles and guardians, waiting for someone worthy to find them.

The stranger—whose name was Marcus—had been one of the guardians. But he had been compromised, hunted by a organization that wanted to use the artifacts for control and power.

That's why he had given the journal to Sarah. She was the only one who could complete the quest.

Chapter 5: Rising Danger

Sarah's phone rang. It was an unknown number.

"Ms. Chen," a cold voice said. "We know you have the journal. We know you found the first artifact. Give them to us, and you can return to your normal life."

"Who are you?" Sarah demanded.

"We are the Consortium. We've been collecting these artifacts for decades. You're interfering with something far beyond your understanding."

Sarah hung up. Her hands were shaking, but her resolve was firm. Whatever the Consortium wanted with these artifacts, it couldn't be good. She had to find the others first.

She opened the journal to the next set of coordinates. Egypt. The pyramids.

Time to keep moving.

Chapter 6: The Confrontation

At the Great Pyramid of Giza, Sarah finally came face to face with the Consortium's agents. Three of them, dressed in black, blocking her path to the entrance she needed.

"Last chance," the leader said. "Hand over what you've found."

Sarah clutched her bag tighter. The orb pulsed inside, as if sensing the danger. She felt its power flowing through her, showing her things she shouldn't be able to know—their weaknesses, their fears, their next moves.

"I don't think so," she said calmly.

What happened next surprised even Sarah. The orb's power erupted from her, creating a barrier of light that sent the agents flying backward. She ran past them, into the pyramid, following the journal's instructions.

Behind her, she heard them shouting, calling for reinforcements. The clock was ticking.

Chapter 7: The Final Choice

Deep beneath the pyramid, Sarah found the chamber the journal described. Seven pedestals stood in a circle, and she now had six of the seven artifacts. Only one remained, somewhere in Tibet.

But the Consortium had followed her. She could hear them approaching, their footsteps echoing through the ancient corridors.

Marcus appeared from the shadows. "Sarah, you've done incredibly well. But now you need to make a choice. Use the six artifacts you have to seal the seventh away forever, or risk everything to complete the set."

"What happens if I complete the set?" Sarah asked.

"You'll have power beyond imagination. You could reshape reality itself. Or you could destroy it trying."

The footsteps grew louder. Sarah had seconds to decide.

She thought about her old life, her bookstore, her simple dreams. Then she thought about the journey she'd been on, the strength she'd discovered within herself, the responsibility she now carried.

"I'll complete the set," she said. "But not for power. To protect it. To make sure the Consortium never gets it."

Marcus smiled. "I hoped you'd say that. Let's get you to Tibet."

The End (of the Rough Draft)
